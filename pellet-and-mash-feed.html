Process Engineering

Detailed Process Overview

Each process stage is engineered with precision equipment to ensure consistent quality, maximum throughput and minimal downtime across your entire production line.

Step 01

Pre-Cleaning

The first step in pelleting is conveying ingredients from silos or storage to batching bins while removing oversized particles, iron contaminants and foreign materials.

  • Rotary Sieve
  • Magnetic Separator
  • Jute Remover
Step 02

Batching

Automatic weighing of ingredients according to feed formulation using PLC-controlled systems.

  • Major & Minor Ingredient Bins
  • Automated Weighing
  • Computerized Reporting
  • Accurate Dosing
Step 03

Grinding

Grinding reduces particle size to achieve maximum surface area for conditioning and pelleting.

  • Uniform Particle Size
  • Lower Energy Consumption
  • Better Pellet Quality
Step 04

Mixing

Ensures uniform distribution of all ingredients throughout the batch.

  • High Mixing Efficiency
  • Low Residue
  • Fast Batch Cycles
Step 05

Conditioning

Heat and moisture are added to improve gelatinization and pellet quality.

  • Steam Conditioning
  • Improved Durability
  • Enhanced Digestibility
Step 06

Pelleting

Material is compressed through precision dies using rollers to form durable pellets.

  • Heavy Duty Pellet Mill
  • High Output Capacity
  • Superior Pellet Quality
Step 07

Cooling

Counterflow coolers remove heat and moisture immediately after pelleting.

  • Longer Shelf Life
  • Uniform Cooling
  • Reduced Breakage
Step 08

Crumbling

Pellets are broken into smaller sizes suitable for chicks and poultry feed.

  • Uniform Crumbs
  • Low Fine Generation
  • Adjustable Roll Gap
Step 09

Screening

Removes fines and oversized material for consistent product quality.

  • High Screening Efficiency
  • Easy Screen Change
  • Recirculation System
Step 10

Bagging

Final product is packed using manual or automatic bagging systems.

  • Electronic Weighing
  • High Accuracy
  • Faster Packing
