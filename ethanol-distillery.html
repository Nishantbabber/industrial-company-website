Overview

Grain processing equipment for distillery operations.

The grain handling and milling section is critical to ethanol production efficiency. We manufacture and supply individual machines and complete lines for cleaning, size reduction, classification and material transfer — configured for maize, rice, barley and other feedstock used in Indian distillery plants.

Equipment range

Machines for grain handling and milling sections.

PRECLEANER

Precleaners are designed to efficiently separate impurities and oversized particles from grains, malt, and other free-flowing materials.

DESTONER

Vacuum-assisted destoner separates stones and heavy impurities from continuous grain flow using vibromotors, calibrated airflow and cyclone discharge.

CLASSIFIER

Our classifier efficiently removes both oversized and undersized impurities from free-flowing materials such as rice, maize, and paddy.

FLOUR SHIFTER

Engineered for flawless performance, our flour sifter employs a precisely calibrated rotating mesh screen and a robust, energy- efficient motor to gently separate and refine flour particles.

HAMMER MILL

In hammer mills, high-speed rotating hammers apply powerful impact force to precisely crush raw materials-producing the particle sizes essential for ethanol production.

BIN ACTIVATOR

Our bin activator is engineered with a robust construction and fully adjustable settings, ensuring seamless compatibility with a wide range of materials — from flour and cement to plastic pellets and beyond.

BLOWER For Dust Exit

Our blower outlet delivers high-volume, high-velocity airflow to efficiently clear dust and maintain optimal air quality.

BUCKET ELEVATOR

Our bucket elevator is meticulously engineered to efficiently lift grains and other free-flowing materials from one point to another.

SCREW CONVEYOR

Our screw conveyors are engineered to handle a variety of granular and powdered materials-such as dal, rice, and grain flour-with efficiency and precision.

WEIGH MACHINE

Ensure reliable, consistent weighing operations through a direct digital interface between the scale and weighing electronics.
